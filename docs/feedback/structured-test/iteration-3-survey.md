# Iteration 3 survey — after knockouts + final

**Goal:** advancement-pick scoring clarity, knockout-bracket comprehension, and the most important question in the entire test: "would you keep using this?"

**When to send:** immediately after Session 3 — once testers have seen their advancement-pick scoring AND seen knockout matches. ~24 hours later, follow up with Survey 2 (steady-state) for the deeper end-state feedback.

**Build instructions:** new Google Form, title "Beta Test — Iteration 3 (knockouts + final thoughts)". Copy the description and questions below.

---

## Form description

> Last short survey of the test — and the most important one. ~2 minutes. Please answer BEFORE you've discussed with the other testers.

---

## Questions

### Q1. Of the picks you made about how teams would finish their groups (winner / runner-up / 3rd-place), were the points awarded clear?

**Type:** Multiple choice (single-select). **Required:** No.

Options:
- Yes — I understood when and why I got points for those picks
- Sort of — I saw points appear but didn't really get when each one was awarded
- No — I'm not sure how those picks were scored
- I don't think I made advancement picks at all

The last option is a deliberate gotcha. If a tester reaches Iteration 3 having never set their advancement picks, that's a sign the picks page isn't surfacing prominently enough during onboarding.

### Q2. If a real version of this app launched tomorrow for World Cup 2026, would you keep using it?

**Type:** Multiple choice (single-select). **Required:** Yes.

Options:
- Yes — I'd use it for the whole tournament
- Probably — depends on what's fixed before launch
- Maybe — I'd try it but I'm not sure I'd stick with it
- Probably not
- No

Add immediately below as a separate field:

### Q2b. (Optional) Why?

**Type:** Paragraph (long text). **Required:** No.

Placeholder: *"What's the deciding factor for you? Even one line is helpful."*

### Q3. What's the ONE thing you'd want changed before you'd recommend it to a friend?

**Type:** Paragraph (long text). **Required:** Yes.

Placeholder: *"Even half a sentence is enough. The most annoying thing — fixing it would have the biggest effect."*

## What this iteration uniquely tells you

- **Q1** is the most reliable check on advancement-pick comprehension. It's the most-misunderstood concept in the app (intermediate scoring, locked at a different time than match predictions, optional but high-leverage). The "I don't think I made any" option exposes onboarding failure.
- **Q2 + Q2b** is *the* single highest-signal datapoint in the entire test. Everything else is in service of being able to interpret this answer.
- **Q3** is the prioritisation list. The same question appears in Survey 2 (Q12) — comparing answers from the two forms tells you whether the tester's "one thing" stayed consistent or shifted as they reflected longer.

## What to do with these answers

Combine with Survey 2 Q12 ("one thing to fix") for your prioritisation list. If 3 of 5 testers say "Probably not" or "No" to Q2, **stop and figure out why before pushing more fixes** — there's a foundational issue, not a polish issue.

## Total length expected

4 fields (Q1 + Q2 + Q2b + Q3). ~2 min for an attentive respondent. Q2b is optional but most testers will fill it in if Q2 was anything other than "Yes."
